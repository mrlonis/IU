INFO-I 101: Week 4 - Lab 1

Database Questions:

  • What are primary keys?
    • Primary keys are unique values that represent a whole row.
  • What are foreign keys?
    • Foreign keys are a type of key that exist in one table but refers to the primary key in another table.
  • What is the differrence between a tuple and an attribute?
    • The difference between a tuple and an attribute is that an attribute the column headings in the relational table. All the attributes together make the Schema and hence the order of the attributes matters. Tuples on the other hand are just a complete row in a table. It is also known as the record and the order of the tuples does not matter.
  • What is SQL (What does it stand for and what does it do?)?
    • SQL stands for Structured Query Language. It is composed of short lines of code that are grouped together. A group of short lines of code is called a query.
  • Provide an example of something that could be used as a primary key (do NOT use the example from the slides) and explain why you chose that.
    • All the employees at a food company are given an employee ID upon being hired. Every employee has a unique set of characters identifying them. I chose this example because I remember getting employee IDs when I was in high school working at fast food restaurant.

More Questions:

  • What is the primary key for the students table?
    • The primary key for the students table is the student id.
  • What are the two foreign keys in the takes_course table?
    • The two foreign keys in the takes_course table is the id and classid elements from the students and courses tables respectively.
  • How are the foreign and primary keys related in the table?
    • The foreign keys and primary keys are related in the table because each student id will correspond to a classid.
  • What is the schema of the courses table
    • The Schema of the courses table is one that relates classids to a name, course number, and a credit hour amount.
